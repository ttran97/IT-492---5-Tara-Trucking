Less-Than-Truckload (LTL) Shipping

LTL shipping, or less-than-truckload shipping, is a transportation method used to move small freight or shipments that do not require a full trailer or truckload. LTL carriers typically pick up multiple shipments from different customers and consolidate them into a single trailer for delivery to different destinations. This allows multiple shippers to share the cost of transportation, making it a cost-effective option for small to mid-sized businesses that do not have the volume to justify a full truckload.

Full Truckload (FTL) Shipping

FTL shipping is used to refer to full truckload shipments, where an entire truck is dedicated to carrying a single shipment from the pickup location to the destination. This is in contrast to less-than-truckload (LTL) shipping, where multiple smaller shipments from different companies are consolidated onto a single truck. FTL offers significant advantages for businesses that require expedited delivery of goods or materials. By using FTL shipping services, companies can reduce transit times, improve supply chain efficiency, and meet tight delivery deadlines.

Intermodal Shipping

Intermodal shipping is a mode of transportation that involves the movement of cargo using multiple modes of transport, such as ships, trucks, and trains, without the need to handle the freight itself when changing modes. The term "intermodal" refers to the use of multiple modes of transportation, or "modes," to move cargo. Intermodal shipping is often used for long-distance cargo transportation, where a combination of modes is necessary to move cargo efficiently and cost-effectively. The most common modes used in intermodal shipping are trucks, trains, and ships, which can be combined to transport cargo over land and sea.
